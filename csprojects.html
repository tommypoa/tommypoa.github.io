CS projects

I was introduced to the world of coding when I took my first CS class in Berkeley. My assumptions were immediately challenged: CS was more about problem solving and creative design than it was about dreary memorization of taught concepts.

Here are my favourite projects that I have worked on in the past year! For more, check out my github .

TransLa

TransLa is a web-based social app that allows users to create, view and share language / culture specific lists of words that are useful for travelling. Think Spotify, but instead of song playlists, there are lists for culture-specific words.

TransLa is developed using Ruby on Rails and styled using HTML/CSS.

Kayak Nav

Kayak Nav is an iOS application that was birthed out of a collaboration with Ahmet, a blind kayaker who embarked on a solo expedition accross the strait of Bosphorus. Kayak Nav takes in marine traffic information (sourced from MarineTraffic) of large ships and informs the user of where any dangerously close ships are with respect to them.

Kayak Nav was started with Python, and subsequently converted to Swift to develop the prototype app.

Maze Room

Maze Room is an auto-generator of a Maze-like room, and has the potential to allow users to control a player around the map. It is complete with basic features such as timestamp and a location status marker.

Maze Room was coded using Java.
